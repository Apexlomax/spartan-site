What is a DAO

A Decentralised Autonomous Organisation (DAO) is unlike any hierarchical controled structure from a traditional organisation. Centralised exchanges are an example of this; you might want a token listed, but besides banding the community together and carrying out a Twitter barrage, you have very little influence on that decision coming to fruition.

DAOs place some control into the hands of the community participants of the platform transparently and programmatically. The power of contracts enforced by mathematics means that a large number of automated actions within a contract can be set up and voted on, the results of which will be automatically actioned. This would mean the community would be able to propose and vote on which tokens they want to be listed. On a successful vote, these tokens would be automatically listed. Resistant to external entities, governments and bad actors getting in the way of that decision.


Spartan DAO

The community should already be familiar with the Spartan Protocol ethos being; "birthed by the community for the community" with the initial developers building the platform the way they wanted to see it within the Binance Smart Chain ecosystem. Chosen for its power, speed and low fees.
Having Spartan Protocol run by a DAO was a central pillar of its development. The intent is to release the DAO in stages to familiarise the community with these powerful decentralised decision-making features.

Spartan Protocol's DAO is open source, which means the code can be viewed by anyone. Moreover, all of the rules and financial transactions are recorded in the Blockchain. This makes the DAO transparent, immutable and most importantly, resistant to corruption.

It's also worth noting that with DAOv2, there will be a "Grant" feature implemented. This will allow the community to send a SPARTA grant to a wallet address. The community may choose to reward the active contributors, or maybe even draw some more in by sending a grant to a trusted community member to share it out as bounties for fixing things, or to develop something new all together.

Only curated pool's LP tokens can be staked in the DaoVault, which is a deliberate decision to drive liquidity into the important pools. More liquidity in the pools means lower slippage for token swaps, which inturn means more incentive for users to utilise the Spartan Pools.

Use your LP Tokens in the DAO

  • Stake them in the DAOVault to gain voting rights with governing the protocol
  • Staking in the DAOVault also enables you to earn extra SPARTA through 'Harvesting'


    • By staking in the DAO, not only will you be eligible to harvest SPARTA yield, but you will also have the usual slip fees + dividends go directly into the liquidity pools which your LP tokens can be redeemed for.

    In order to incentivise community participation in the DAO, SPARTA emissions will be directed to stakers through the DAOVault contract. You will in real-time accrue SPARTA that can be harvested.

    DAO Weight

    The community continues to drive Spartan Protocol and if you want to join the DAO, it is hoped that you join it with the intent to see it succeed, grow and prosper for the LONG TERM. We should focus on proposals that will benefit the platform in the long run, that means focusing on things that help it grow, develop and improve reach before focusing on individual benefit. Community value will drive results in individual benefit, it most likely won't work the other way around.


    Get involved and influence the direction of Spartan Protocol

    To have your say with proposing assets you must be providing weight in the DAO. To do this you must:

  • Provide liquidity to the pools — When you add liquidity to a pool you will receive LP tokens representing your weight in the relative pool.
  • Lock LP tokens in the DaoVault

    • You can either vote on current proposals or put forward your own. View the current pending proposal and vote if you agree with it. Voting in support of a proposal does not attract any fees other than "gas" which thanks to BSC is insignificant when compared to other chains.



    Proposals from anyone in the community

    If you want to submit a new proposal there is a SPARTA fee for it to be listed. This is done to discourage spam proposals and ensure the proposal being put forward has been thought about. The fee is allocated to the RESERVE contract which will eventually be emitted back out via incentives.

    If a proposal reaches it's required consensus (50% or 66% depending on the severity of the proposed action), it can be switched over to "finalising" status by anyone calling the smart contract function, where it will sit for the "CoolOffPeriod" (currently set at 3 days). After the "CoolOffPeriod" has passed, anyone can attempt to finalise the proposal where it will recalculate and if still above 50% consensus will automatically run the required function/s to make the proposed action happen.

    If you want to vote against the proposal all you need to do is….nothing. Well, except ensuring you have some weight in the DAO that is NOT voted "for" the proposal. By not voting you are voting "no" on the proposal. The intent is that proposals will need to be actively communicated within the community channels on telegram, debated, refined and then collectivity moved forward. The majority of the community will need to support the proposal to get it to move forward.

    How to Vote

    Once you have decided to jump on board, you will be able to have your say and help to steer the project towards a prosperous future. You will need to stake your LP tokens into the DAOVault.

    The higher the liquidity you provide to the protocol, the more liquidity tokens you will have to be able to lock in the DAO and therefore the more voting power that you will have for the direction of the platform.

    The DAO proposal page will allow you to interact with the DAO proposals. This is where you can either vote towards current proposals or even create your own. Together, the community will drive this project forwards.
