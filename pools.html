Liquidity Pools Design

Spartan Protocol enables users to add liquidity to pools for BEP20 assets on Binance Smart Chain to expose their assets to yield generating revenue.

The liquidity pools contain two assets each that are incentivized to be balanced in $USD value by users swapping between the assets in the pool.

Base asset (SPARTA)

Paired asset (BEP20)

As touched on above; the assets held in the liquidity pools are economically driven to be equal in USD value. By pairing the BEP20 tokens with SPARTA we have a common asset to price all other assets in. Therefore there is no reliance on external oracles to calculate the value of one asset against another.



When someone swaps between two assets in a pool, they add one asset and remove the other at a rate defined by how much of each asset exist in the pool. To resist manipulation, there is an algorithm which scales the rate exponentially when the trade size is a larger portion of the overall pool's size. Therefore pool manipulators pay very high fees (making the pools manipulation-resistant) The difference between the spot rate and end-rate is called the 'slip'. This goes directly into the pool to incentivize liquidity providers. 100% of all pool-fees are directed to the liquidity providers.

LP Token Utility

When users add SPARTA & BEP20 assets to the liquidity pools; they receive LP tokens in exchange. How LP tokens can be utilized:

  • You could of course simply HODl them whilst they collect 'Slip Fees' & 'Dividends'
  • Ideally though, you stake them in the DAOVault to gain voting rights with governing the protocol
  • Staking in DAOVault also enables you to earn extra SPARTA yield through 'Harvesting'
  • When you are ready to remove your liquidity, you can redeem them for a percentage of assets from the pool (Remove liquidity)

    • LP Token Utility - Redemption Value

    Each LP token has redemption value calculated quite simply using the base asset (SPARTA). When you remove liquidity from a pool, you turn in the LP tokens and receive a calculated quantity of the relative BEP20 asset along with SPARTA. The calculation involves dividing the quantity of LP tokens being redeemed by the total amount in existence (this gives us the % of the pool represented by those LP tokens) Quite simply; you are entitled to get that percentage of the total assets in the pool upon redemption of those LP tokens.

    LP Token Utility - Slip Fees & Dividends

    At the time of writing this, there are two major methods driving incentives into the liquidity pools, both involve driving SPARTA into one side of the pool; causing a small arbitrage opportunity and therefore swap activity and more incentives:

    Dividends

    Pools that are in the 'Curated' list are eligible to receive SPARTA dividends from the RESERVE contract. The SPARTA dividends being injected into the pools will help offset some concerns that liquidity providers might have about impermanent loss and provide great benefits to liquidity providers. Read more about dividends here.

    Slip Fees

    Every time someone places a swap trade using the pools, there is a slip-based fee calculated, then taken during the trade and sent directly to that pool's balance of held assets. These fees grow the total pooled asset balance and therefore the redemption value of each LP token in that pool.

    LP Token Utility - Stake in DAOVault

    LP Tokens can be staked in the DAO to provide the user with voting weight on proposals in the DAO. An important aspect of Spartan Protocol is it's community-programmable nature. The DAO will be able to control a significant amount of variables and functions and help shape the future of the protocol. DAO weight obtained from staking also enables the user to visit the DAO as often as they like to 'Harvest' SPARTA rewards directly to their wallet. More on the DAO here.

    Assess Your Liquidity Position

    Now that I have added liquidity and my assets are working hard to earn revenue from dividends and slip fees. What do I do next?

    'Assess those GAINZ!'

    You are probably wondering how?

    Positions Page

    Contributors are working on a positions page for the V2 pools to assess your position via different scopes:

  • How much of each asset they have ‘added’ to the pools
  • How much of each asset they have ‘removed’ from the pools
  • Bond asset ‘adds’
  • The current redemption value of their LP holdings
  • Harvest earnings

    • Using all that, you will be able to assess your position in both 'vs holding $USD' and 'vs holding same tokens'. Users may opt to shift their liquidity to a higher-performing pool or play out whatever strategy makes sense based on the data they have & their preferred held assets.
